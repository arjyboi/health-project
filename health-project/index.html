Welcome

This website is about the reasons why you shouldn't start tobacco or quit your tobacco usage. Enjoy your visit!

Long-Term Effects

Long-term effects can include:

  • Smoking can cause cancer, heart disease, stroke, lung diseases, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Smoking can cause certain eye diseases
  • Smoking can cause cancer almost anywhere in your body
  • Tobacco kills half of the people that don't quit, the other half live miserably
  • Addiction
  • Studies show that 3 out of 4 people who smoke in high school grow up to be adults who smoke, even if they intend to quit in a few years

What is tobacco

Tobacco is a plant used in cigarettes. It contains nicotine which is addictive, and can cause depression. Street names can include tobacco, cigarettes, cigs, smokes, darts, chew, spit, snuff, dip, shisha, and hookah. You can either smoke or chew tobacco

Why do people use tobacco

Reasons:

  • Most people started as teenagers that thought it was cool or just wanted to try it
  • The tobacco industry spends billions of dollars every year in ads
  • Studies show that young people who see smoking in movies are more likely to smoke
  • Addiction means repeating something regardless of the harmful effects that follow
  • Addiction means emotional or mental dependance on a particular substance
  • Nicotine is thought to be as addictive as cocaine or heroin

Tips to quit

  • CDC: Tips From Former Smokers - Terrie H.'s Voice Tip Ad
  • Don't start vaping, vaping is less harmful than smoking but it's still very dangerous
  • In February 2020, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) confirmed 2,807 cases of e-cigarette or vaping use-associated lung injury (EVALI) and 68 deaths attributed to that condition.
  • Research says that vaping is bad for your heart and lungs
  • Vaping is just as addictive as e-cigarettes, vapes still has nicotine (nicotine effects in Nicotine effects paragraph)
  • Call 811 (Ontario, Canada)
  • Quit gradually
  • Talk to your doctor about over-the-counter drugs to help you quit

Nicotine effects

  • Dizziness
  • Nausea
  • Headache
  • Diarrhea
  • Tachycardia (unusually high heart rate)
  • Cancer
  • Cardiovascular diseases (relating to lungs and heart)
  • COPD (restricted breathing)
  • Cravings
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Xerostomia (dry mouth)
  • Increased blood pressure
  • Indigestion
  • Mild stimulation
  • Possible blood restriction
  • Abdominal pain
  • Ulcers (a literal hole in your stomach)

What happens when you quit

  • 1 to 9 months: You cough less and your lungs work better
  • 1 year: Your risk of heart disease or strokes caused by smoking is cut in half
  • 5 years: Your risk of stroke are similar to someone who has never touched a cigarette or vape
  • 10 years: Your risk of dying of lung cancer is so much lower
  • 15 years: Your risk of heart disease is similar to someone who has never smoked before

    • Remember, quitting is really hard and it's not an immediate thing, but your future self is going to thank you
